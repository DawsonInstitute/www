Our Mission

The Dawson Institute is committed to advancing fundamental physics through rigorous research, open and collaborative science, and the responsible application of new technologies to benefit society. Our scope is intentionally broad to support growth across energy sciences, spacetime and quantum field theory, computational physics, materials science, and related interdisciplinary areas. Current work is led by the founder; collaborations and inquiries are welcome.

About our name: The Institute is named in honor of John M. Dawson (1930–2001), a pioneering computational physicist and the father of plasma-based acceleration.

Explore our outputs on GitHub and our curated publications and datasets at the Dawson Institute community on Zenodo.

Publications & Software

We publish preprints, release software repositories, and share datasets openly when possible. Below are links to our main discovery hubs and a short list of representative outputs.

  • Zenodo community: Dawson Institute on Zenodo — publications, preprints, and datasets.
  • GitHub organization: github.com/DawsonInstitute — code, tools, and reproducible workflows.
  • Representative repositories and outputs:
    • Repo: cosmological-constant-workbench — a falsifiability-oriented, reproducible workbench that integrates an LQG-inspired vacuum-energy predictor and evaluates its implied vacuum energy via bounded parameter scans. A 108-point scan finds the predictor exceeds the observed dark-energy density by ≳150 orders of magnitude, yielding an empirical no‑go style constraint. Paper & PDF: LQG CC Constraints (Zenodo).
    • Repo: coherence-gravity-coupling — coherence-modulated gravity validation and null results for curvature–EM couplings. Includes systematic exclusion limits (κ_R < 5×10¹⁷ m² for lab terrestrial conditions) and a reproducible CLI analysis pipeline (GitHub).
    • Repo: hts-coils — high-temperature superconductor coil designs and simulation tools (GitHub).
    • Repo: irrotational-warp-lab — validation and optimization framework for irrotational shift-vector warp metrics. GPU-accelerated 3D volume integration, Bayesian optimization (5× efficiency gain), and reproduction of Rodal (2025) convergence results. Paper pipeline with LaTeX + figure generation (GitHub).
