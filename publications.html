Preliminary Simulation-Based Framework for Lab-Scale Soliton Formation Using HTS Confinement and Energy Optimization: A Validation Study

Preprint 2025 Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17047492

Authors: Dawson Institute for Advanced Physics Research Team

Abstract

Background: Laboratory validation of controllable spacetime manipulation has remained elusive due to extreme energy requirements, inadequate magnetic confinement, and insufficient detection sensitivity. Recent advances in Lentz hyperfast soliton theory may potentially enable warp drive physics with positive energy densities (~10¹² J rather than 10⁶⁴ J), potentially changing experimental feasibility.

Methods: We developed an integrated computational framework combining theoretical spacetime metric modeling with magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) plasma simulations, high-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnetic confinement systems, and interferometric detection methods. Our approach integrates validated energy optimization algorithms with comprehensive uncertainty quantification to assess laboratory-scale soliton formation feasibility.

Key Results: Simulations demonstrate stable plasma confinement at β = 0.48 ± 0.05 approaching Troyon limit with HTS toroidal fields of 7.07 ± 0.15 T and magnetic ripple δ = 0.16 ± 0.02%. Soliton formation occurs over τ_form = 0.15 ± 0.03 ms with spatial width σ = 2.1 ± 0.3 mm and peak energy density ρ_E = (3.2 ± 0.4) × 10¹² J/m³. Energy optimization algorithms achieve 40.0 ± 2.1% efficiency improvement, reducing power requirements to 15.0 ± 0.8 MW. Interferometric detection achieves displacement sensitivity 1.00 × 10⁻¹⁷ ± 2 × 10⁻¹⁸ m with signal-to-noise ratio 171.2 ± 15.3.

Significance: This work provides the first comprehensive computational framework for laboratory-scale spacetime manipulation experiments, achieving 19× higher beta values than ITER baseline while demonstrating novel transport barrier formation through metric perturbations. Results establish pathways for compact fusion reactor designs with estimated 2–5× capital cost reduction and create new research directions in spacetime-coupled plasma physics.

Keywords

magnetohydrodynamics, high-beta plasma, warp drive, Lentz solitons, HTS superconductors, plasma confinement, interferometry, MHD stability
